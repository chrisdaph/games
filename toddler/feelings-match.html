About Feelings Match

Feelings Match helps toddlers put a name to what they feel inside. Eight friendly faces, happy, sad, mad, scared, surprised, calm, excited, and worried, are matched to the word for that feeling. Once all eight are paired, a short kid-friendly description of each feeling appears to read together. It plays free in any browser, great for home, preschool, or a quiet moment before nap time.

How to Play

  1. Tap a face on the left.
  2. Tap the word on the right that names that feeling.
  3. Correct matches lock in and the progress bar fills up.
  4. Keep matching until all eight feelings are paired!

Why Naming Feelings Matters

Before a child can manage a big feeling, they first need the words for it. Naming an emotion, "I feel mad" instead of just acting mad, is one of the earliest and most useful steps in emotional regulation, and it is a skill that grows with practice.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you play Feelings Match?

Tap a feeling face on the left, then tap the word on the right that names it. Correct pairs lock in until all eight are matched.

What does Feelings Match teach?

It builds emotional vocabulary, helping children name and recognize feelings like happy, sad, mad, and worried.

Is Feelings Match free to play?

Yes. It plays free in any web browser with nothing to download or install.
